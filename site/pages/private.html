Personal Computing

Apple I

Computers have multiples uses. They are commonly being used by everyone. Thanks to some important machines that have come trough the years. A notable example who set the sucess of pple was the originally named Apple I. It was different from most of the time because it came ready to use. You just needed to plug in a TV. It was designed by Steve Wozniak, surnamed Woz. The computer was also different from most for another reason, it was uncompatible with others. Setting the long fame of Apple wanting to control every aspect of their products. It came with a MOS 6502 @ 1 MHz CPU and 4KB of memory. It also came with an OS specially made for it, called Apple DOS. It was a command line interface. The price was interestingly placed at 666.66$. Because Woz liked recursive numbers. He later said he was unaware of the connotations with the devil number. It was superseeded by the Apple II, and Apple III.

Apple Lisa

The Lisa was said to stand for Local Integrated Architecture by the engineers. However, Steve Jobs later confirmed it actually was named after his daugther Lisa Brennan-Jobs. After Jobs discovered that the Xerox PARC had a GUI, it was redesigned to support one.

The Macintosh

The Macintosh computers is the longest series of computers Apple has. They are known today as Mac. They were an important milestone for personal computers as it offered a GUI and a mouse to interact with it. Many design elements of the GUI are recognizable in todays MacOS like the menu bar and finder.
